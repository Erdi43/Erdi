Discover the beauty and culture of this Balkan country

Introduction

North Macedonia, officially the Republic of North Macedonia, is a country in Southeast Europe. It gained independence in 1991 as one of the successor states of Yugoslavia. Skopje is the capital and largest city.

Geography

North Macedonia is a landlocked country bordering Kosovo to the northwest, Serbia to the north, Bulgaria to the east, Greece to the south, and Albania to the west. The country is known for its mountainous terrain, including the Å ar Mountains in the northwest.

Culture

North Macedonia has a rich cultural heritage influenced by both its Slavic roots and Ottoman past. Traditional Macedonian music, dance, and cuisine are integral parts of the country's identity. Ohrid, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is famous for its ancient churches and stunning lake views.

Tourism

Tourists visiting North Macedonia can explore its historical sites, picturesque landscapes, and vibrant cities. Must-see attractions include Skopje's Old Bazaar, Lake Ohrid, Matka Canyon, and the ancient city of Bitola. The country offers a unique blend of history, nature, and hospitality.
